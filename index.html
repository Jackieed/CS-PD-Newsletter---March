Executive Summary

March marked a significant milestone in our product delivery approach — with the Scope V3 ReDesign launch, the Clinic Ambassador Pilot, and the start of our Zendesk Workflow Optimization initiative, the team demonstrated our commitment to thoughtful, user-centered delivery at every layer.

🚀 Major Wins
What We Shipped in March

Clinic Ambassador Pilot Program

We identified and engaged super users at each clinic to preview the Scope V3 redesign ahead of wider deployment. These ambassadors became internal champions — providing early feedback and driving smoother adoption across the medical group. The model reduced friction during launch, accelerated feature adoption, and creates a scalable template for future product rollouts.

ScopeAI V3 ReDesign Launch (Mar 26–31)

A major UX overhaul of the AI intake workflow for Medical Assistants — featuring a redesigned landing page with left-panel navigation, real-time progress tracking, session statistics, and AI Brief as the default view. Cross-functional delivery with Engineering, Product, Design, and CS/AI Implementation. Reception was overwhelmingly positive with minimal production issues.

Zendesk Workflow Optimization Initiative

Kicked off a partnership with Zendesk’s dedicated account manager to streamline our end-to-end support flow — from ticket creation through Jira tracking to production resolution. Working sessions and phased implementation are planned for April & May.

Capella EHR Deprecation — Quickbase Rollout Complete

Successfully transitioned all patient communication and task management from the legacy Capella EHR to Quickbase Tasks. Deployed to all 45+ current providers across CMG/OFMG and NYC teams — a critical milestone in our broader EHR consolidation strategy.
